Welcome

Over the past few decades, space telescopes such as Kepler, K2, and TESS have observed millions of stars to discover worlds outside our solar system. These planets, called exoplanets, are primarily detected using the transit method.

This project combines these astronomical data with the power of artificial intelligence. This way, anyone can explore, analyze and discover how the models that help identify possible new planets in real NASA data work.

This platform is not only a scientific tool, it is also a window into the future of space exploration: bringing the search for other worlds closer to everyone.

Discoveries in numbers

  • +5,500 exoplanets confirmed to date (NASA, 2025).
  • Thousands of planetary candidates still under review, which could become future discoveries.
