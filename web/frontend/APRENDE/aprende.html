‚ú® The transit method explained

A planet that crosses in front of its star blocks a tiny fraction of light. Detecting that dip in brightness is like seeing a distant shadow in the universe.

The resulting light curve from a planetary transit provides key data such as the planet's size (related to the depth of the brightness dip) and the orbital period (related to the time between transits).

Kepler

Pioneer in the exoplanet hunt. It used a single fixed region of the sky to search for thousands of planets, confirming over 2,600.

K2

Extension of the original Kepler mission, observing new sky regions after the telescope's reaction wheel failure.

TESS

The present of exploration. It is surveying nearby bright stars for follow-up studies, covering nearly the entire sky.
